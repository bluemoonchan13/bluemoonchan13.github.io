Daylighting

This project was to demonstrate the daylighting effects on materials, size of space, and limitations. The model is a shoebox sized box that is made from posterboard and several materials like scrapbook paper, sandpaper, balsa wood, and stains and spray paint. The goal was to create a space that was realistic looking and would imitate the effects that daylight would have on real world materials.

I needed to see how different materials affected the brightness of the space and how the daylight either deadens or expands in the space. So I experimented on several materials in order to understand how different materials can affect it in different ways. The challenge was to create a space that created a bright space while still using materials that I would use in the real world.
